Calico Combat League - Audio Style Guide
==========================================

CORE IDENTITY
-------------
The league should sound organized, competitive, warm, and slightly theatrical:
a real regional combat promotion with a distinctive calico-orange personality.
Use recurring percussion, bass, and short brass motifs to connect venues. Avoid
licensed samples, recognizable melodies, crowd chants with intelligible words,
and sounds that compete with combat impacts.

All tempo ranges are creative targets, not gameplay requirements. Music should
loop cleanly and should reserve headroom for UI and fight SFX.

MAIN MENU
Mood: Confident, welcoming, anticipatory.
Tempo: 88-96 BPM.
Instruments: Warm bass, tight drums, muted brass, clean electric guitar.
Energy: 2/5. Establish the league without exhausting the player.

BOXING GYM
Mood: Gritty, focused, old-school work ethic.
Tempo: 100-108 BPM.
Instruments: Dry kick/snare, upright or electric bass, muted trumpet, handclaps.
Energy: 3/5. Steady forward motion with a disciplined pulse.

MUAY THAI GYM
Mood: Precise, agile, respectful, dangerous.
Tempo: 108-116 BPM.
Instruments: Hand drums, small cymbals, plucked strings, restrained reed lead.
Energy: 3/5. Layered rhythm should suggest eight-limb movement, not caricature.

WRESTLING GYM
Mood: Heavy, relentless, grounded.
Tempo: 96-104 BPM.
Instruments: Low toms, bass guitar, low strings, stomps, short horn pulses.
Energy: 3/5. Weight and repetition should imply pressure and control.

BJJ ACADEMY
Mood: Patient, technical, quietly tense.
Tempo: 82-92 BPM.
Instruments: Soft hand percussion, acoustic bass, marimba or muted plucks, pads.
Energy: 2/5. Leave space and build tension through subtle interlocking patterns.

CHAMPIONSHIP GYM
Mood: Elite, imposing, prestigious.
Tempo: 112-120 BPM.
Instruments: Hybrid drums, low brass, cinematic strings, restrained synth pulse.
Energy: 4/5. Reuse the league motif in a larger, more serious arrangement.

BATTLE
Mood: Urgent, kinetic, broadcast-ready.
Tempo: 124-132 BPM.
Instruments: Punchy drums, synth bass, brass stabs, rhythmic low strings.
Energy: 5/5. Maintain momentum without masking impacts or tiring the listener.

VICTORY
Mood: Earned relief, momentum, celebration.
Tempo: 118-126 BPM.
Instruments: Bright brass, full drums, rising bass, concise league motif.
Energy: 4/5. Resolve quickly into a loop that can sit under reward reading.

DEFEAT
Mood: Disappointed but determined, never humiliating.
Tempo: 68-78 BPM.
Instruments: Sparse piano, low pad, soft floor tom, subdued bass.
Energy: 1/5. Keep it brief and leave emotional room for another attempt.

CHAMPION CELEBRATION
Mood: Triumphant, historic, unmistakably league championship.
Tempo: 128-136 BPM.
Instruments: Brass fanfare, full drums, cymbal lifts, strings, league motif.
Energy: 5/5. The largest arrangement in the game, festive rather than ominous.

MIXING TARGETS
--------------
- Deliver music around -3 dBFS peak with consistent perceived loudness.
- Keep the midrange open for impacts and future announcer voice if ever desired.
- Avoid excessive sub-bass that translates poorly on phone speakers.
- Check every track on phone speakers, earbuds, and at low master volume.
- Keep SFX transient and centered; music may use a wider stereo image.
- Do not bake fades into looping files. Runtime transitions provide the fades.
